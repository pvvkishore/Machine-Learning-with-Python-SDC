# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our
community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and
orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

* **Being Respectful**: Using welcoming and inclusive language
* **Being Collaborative**: Working together and valuing diverse perspectives
* **Being Patient**: Understanding that everyone learns at their own pace
* **Being Helpful**: Offering assistance to those who need it
* **Being Open-Minded**: Accepting constructive criticism gracefully
* **Focusing on Learning**: Prioritizing what is best for the community and educational goals
* **Showing Empathy**: Being kind and understanding towards others
* **Giving Credit**: Acknowledging contributions and sources appropriately

### Examples of unacceptable behavior:

* Use of sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention
* Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* Public or private harassment of any kind
* Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
* Plagiarism or submitting others' work as your own
* Sharing course materials outside the intended educational context without permission
* Disruptive behavior in learning spaces (online or offline)
* Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional or educational setting

## Educational Environment Standards

As this is an educational repository, we also emphasize:

### Academic Integrity

* **Do Your Own Work**: Submit your own solutions to assignments
* **Cite Sources**: Always credit code, ideas, or resources you use
* **Collaborate Appropriately**: Group work is encouraged where specified, but individual assignments should be completed individually
* **Ask for Help Properly**: It's okay to ask for guidance, but not to ask for complete solutions
* **Respect Learning Process**: Avoid sharing complete assignment solutions publicly

### Constructive Learning

* **Ask Questions**: No question is too simple or "stupid"
* **Share Knowledge**: Help others when you can
* **Learn from Mistakes**: Treat errors as learning opportunities
* **Provide Feedback**: Give constructive, helpful feedback
* **Be Patient**: Remember everyone has different backgrounds and learning speeds

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Course instructors and repository maintainers are responsible for clarifying and
enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They will take appropriate and
fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate,
threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject
comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are
not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for
moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

* GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions)
* Course forums and communication channels
* Classroom (physical or virtual)
* Course-related events and workshops
* Any space where you are representing the course or community

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in
public spaces. Examples include using an official e-mail address, posting via
an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative.

## Reporting Violations

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns,
please report it by:

1. **For Course-Related Issues**: Contact course instructors through official university channels
2. **For Repository Issues**: 
   - Email the repository maintainers
   - Use GitHub's report feature
3. **For Serious Violations**: Contact KL University's student conduct office

### What to Include

When reporting, please provide:

* Your contact information
* Names (real, nicknames, or pseudonyms) of those involved
* Description of what happened
* When and where it occurred
* Any additional context or evidence
* Whether you prefer anonymity

### Confidentiality

All reports will be handled with discretion. We will respect confidentiality
requests to protect victims of abuse.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining
the consequences for any action deemed in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed
unprofessional or unwelcome.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from maintainers, providing clarity
around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was
inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No
interaction with the people involved for a specified period of time. This
includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels.
Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including
sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public
communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or
private interaction with the people involved is allowed during this period.
Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community
standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an
individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the
community.

### Academic Consequences

For violations of academic integrity:

* **First Offense**: Warning and required resubmission
* **Second Offense**: Zero on assignment
* **Serious/Repeated Offenses**: Referral to university academic integrity board

## Appeals Process

If you believe you have been falsely or unfairly accused of violating this Code
of Conduct, you may appeal by:

1. Submitting a written appeal to the course instructors
2. Providing your perspective on the incident
3. Including any relevant evidence or context

Appeals will be reviewed within a reasonable timeframe.
